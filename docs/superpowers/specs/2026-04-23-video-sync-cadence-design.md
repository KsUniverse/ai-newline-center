# Video Sync Cadence Design

**Date:** 2026-04-23  
**Scope:** Adjust active-account video sync cadence after a newly discovered video.

## Goal

Change the active video sync strategy so a newly discovered video reduces the near-term polling frequency, then gradually increases frequency again as the account approaches its next learned publish window.

## Design

Keep the current `scheduler -> planner -> queue -> worker -> profile state` architecture unchanged and modify only the account sync cadence calculation in `account-video-sync-profile.service.ts`.

- Remove the current behavior where a newly discovered video enters an immediate 10-minute fast-follow loop.
- Keep the success state learning logic intact, including temporary windows and historical publish-window learning.
- Reinterpret `fastFollowUntil` as a short post-publish cooldown marker rather than an aggressive recheck marker.
- During that post-publish cooldown, if the next historical publish window is still far away, schedule the next sync at 60 minutes.
- When the account gets closer to its next historical publish window, gradually increase sync frequency:
  - 120 to 60 minutes before the next window: 60 minutes
  - 60 to 30 minutes before the next window: 30 minutes
  - within 30 minutes before the next window, or already inside the window: 10 minutes
- Preserve existing low-activity, cooldown, banned, and failure-backoff rules.

## Behavior

- New video just discovered outside the next window: frequency drops to 1 hour instead of jumping to 10 minutes.
- As the next likely publish window approaches, active accounts step back up to 30 minutes and then 10 minutes.
- Accounts still remain adaptive through learned windows and temporary windows generated by out-of-window discoveries.

## Testing

- Add tests for post-publish cooldown behavior away from the next window.
- Add tests for staged ramp-up near the next learned publish window.
- Keep existing low-activity and failure tests passing.

## Risks

- The next-window detection must work with both learned and temporary windows.
- Frequency changes must not regress the existing success/failure state transitions.
